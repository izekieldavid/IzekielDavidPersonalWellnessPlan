SOCIAL WELLNESS

Social wellbeing refers to the interactions we have with people, and how we communicate. During challenging times our relationships can provide help. Social wellbeing means creating safe, caring, and positive relationships, and cultivating a meaningful bond with those around you. Other people who don't want to be socially active tend to be lonely and forget that they always have someone to depend on. Sometimes loneliness can lead to symptoms such as depression. To avoid this circumstances remember to make an effort to keep in touch with supportive friends and family. Participate in group discussions and practice active listening. Start small if you're too insecure, it can be your closest friend or even a circle of friends.
